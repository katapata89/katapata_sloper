What is the reduced-size sample PDF for?

KATAPATA can export a reduced-size sample PDF for free. This file is not meant to be used as a full-scale sewing pattern. It is for checking the shape of the sloper and the general look of the PDF output.

What you can check with the sample PDF

  • The overall shape of the sloper you created.
  • How the selected pieces, such as front bodice, back bodice, sleeve, skirt, or pants, appear in the PDF.
  • The look of the lines and labels after saving as a PDF.
  • The basic output flow before purchasing full-scale output.

It is not full scale

The reduced-size sample PDF is not the correct size for cutting fabric. Even if you print it, it will not print at actual size, so please do not use it directly as a sewing pattern.

When you want to print a sloper for actual use, export the regular-size PDF or the A4 tiled PDF.

Use it as a check before purchase

Before paid output, it is helpful to check the reduced-size sample PDF first. You can confirm that the entered measurements look right, that you selected the pieces you want, and that the overall balance does not feel unusual.

This makes it easier to review the data before exporting a full-scale PDF.

How it differs from regular-size and A4 tiled PDFs

The regular-size PDF and A4 tiled PDF are for full-scale use. The regular-size PDF is suitable for checking the full-size drafting data or printing with a large-format printer. The A4 tiled PDF is designed for printing on a home printer and joining the pages together.

Both full-scale PDF types are available after purchase. Before printing, make sure the print scale is set to 100%.
