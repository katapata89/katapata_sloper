Guide

What is a sloper?

A sloper is one of the most basic shapes used in garment making. It is not a finished blouse, dress, or pants pattern. Instead, it works like a foundation based on body measurements and balance.

A sloper is a starting point for pattern making

When making clothes, you may add ease, change the length, adjust the neckline, choose a sleeve shape, or add style lines. Before making those design choices, it helps to have a simple base shape that is close to the body.

A sloper is that base. It is not only about making something tight to the body. It gives you a reference point, so you can see where and how much you want to change the shape for a design.

What you can create with KATAPATA

With KATAPATA, you can enter measurements and draft slopers for the bodice, sleeve, skirt, and pants on screen. You can check the shapes, make adjustments where needed, and export them as PDFs.

If drafting directly on paper feels intimidating, KATAPATA lets you check the overall shape on screen first.

From a sloper to a garment

After creating a sloper, you use it as the base for the garment you want to make. For example, to make a blouse, you would think about ease, length, neckline, and sleeve shape. To make a dress, you would decide how the bodice and skirt connect and what kind of silhouette you want.

In other words, a sloper is not the finished item. It is more like the first map for starting your pattern-making process.
