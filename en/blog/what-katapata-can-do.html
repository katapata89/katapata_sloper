Guide

What KATAPATA can and can’t do

KATAPATA is a browser-based tool for drafting sewing slopers. This page summarizes what you can do with KATAPATA and what you will still need to do after creating a sloper.

What KATAPATA can do

  • Automatically draft slopers from entered measurements.
  • Show bodice, sleeve, skirt, and pants slopers on screen.
  • Let you adjust some parts of the shapes on screen when needed.
  • Export a free reduced-size sample PDF so you can check the general shape.
  • After purchase, export regular-size PDFs and A4 tiled PDFs.
  • Load saved data so you can check data you created before.

What KATAPATA does not do by itself

  • Automatically create finished blouse, dress, or other garment patterns.
  • Automatically develop design details such as collars, pockets, openings, gathers, or other style elements.
  • Automatically add seam allowances or detailed sewing instructions.
  • Completely replace fitting checks or test sewing.

Sloper drafting and garment pattern making are different steps

KATAPATA is made for creating the sloper that becomes the foundation of garment making. After the sloper is created, you develop it according to the garment you want to make, such as by adding ease, changing the length, shaping the neckline, choosing sleeves, or adding style lines.

So the PDF exported from KATAPATA should be understood as a starting point for pattern making, not as a finished sewing pattern for a completed garment.

When KATAPATA is useful

KATAPATA is useful when you want to create a sloper from your own measurements, check the shape before drafting on paper, or keep a PDF version of your sloper.

It is designed to be simple enough for people who are learning the sloper-making process, while still being useful for people who already have experience with sewing and pattern making.
