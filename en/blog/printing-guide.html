Things to check before printing your PDF

When printing a PDF exported from KATAPATA, the print scale is very important. If the scale changes even a little, the size of the sloper will change too.

Set the print scale to 100%

In the print dialog, check that settings such as “fit to page,” “shrink to printable area,” or “scale to paper size” are not selected. For full-scale use, print at 100%.

The wording may vary depending on your printer or PDF viewer. When in doubt, choose the setting closest to “actual size” or “100%.”

Test-print one page first

Before printing every page, it is a good idea to print just one test page and check whether the guide marks or scale are correct.

If there is a 5 cm guide, measure it with a ruler and make sure it is exactly 5 cm. If it is not, the print settings may not be set to actual size.

When using the A4 tiled PDF

The A4 tiled PDF is made for home printers. After printing, you join the pages together by following the trim marks, margins, and page numbers.

Before taping the pages together, check that all pages are present and that the print scale did not change partway through printing.

The reduced-size sample PDF is not for full-scale checking

The free reduced-size sample PDF is for checking the shape and general output style. It cannot be used to confirm actual size or to cut fabric.

For actual use, export the regular-size PDF or the A4 tiled PDF.
